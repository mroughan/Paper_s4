    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/2104.04135v1" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>A qualitative and quantitative extension of the chaotic models used to generate self-similar traffic with long-range dependence (LRD) is presented by means of the formulation of a model that considers the use of piecewise affine one-dimensional maps.     <title>Modeling Self-Similar Traffic for Network Simulation</title>
    <updated>2013-08-18T07:46:14Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1308.3842v1" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/1308.3842v1" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>In order to closely simulate the real network scenario thereby verify the effectiveness of protocol designs, it is necessary to model the traffic flows carried over realistic networks. Extensive studies [1] showed that the actual traffic in access and local area networks (e.g., those generated by ftp and video streams) exhibits the property of self-similarity and long-range dependency (LRD) [2]. In this appendix we briefly introduce the property of self-similarity and suggest a practical approach for modeling self-similar traces with specified traffic intensity.</summary>

    <title>Irregular sets for piecewise monotonic maps</title>
    <updated>2021-01-14T03:58:28Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1912.12081v3" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/1912.12081v3" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>For any transitive piecewise monotonic map for which the set of periodic measures is dense in the set of ergodic invariant measures (such as monotonic mod one transformations and piecewise monotonic maps with two monotonic pieces), we show that the set of points for which the Birkhoff average of a continuous function does not exist (called the irregular set) is either empty or has full topological entropy. This generalizes Thompson's theorem for irregular sets of $β$-transformations, and reduces a complete description of irregular sets of transitive piecewise monotonic maps to Hofbauer-Raith problem on the density of periodic measures.</summary>
